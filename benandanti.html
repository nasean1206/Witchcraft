The Benandanti

The Benandanti, or the "Good Walkers" were a peasant fertility cult in Friuli. born with the caul over their head, the "Good Walkers" were a select group of men who were destined to fall into a deep slumber during a few days of the year as a pseudo-military service to their society. The four days of the year, labeled the Ember Days, saw these "Good Walkers" traverse into the night realm, leaving their physical bodies behind, to battle aganist antagonistic witches in order to secure the fate of the crop harvest. A triumph yielded a fertile harvest; however, a defeat foreshadowed a harrowing famine.

This fertility cult came to the attention of the Friulian Church authorities in 1575 when witchcraft accusations were filed toward Paolo Gasparotto. Gasparotto among locals were interrogated by Roman Catholic Inquisitors. Under the pressure by the Inquisition, the Benandanti allowed their noctural rituals to be attributed to the diabolical stereotype of the witches' Sabbath. The Benandanti were soon condemned for their heretical acts of witchcraft, which eventually wiped them out from Friuli by the 17th century.
